# Keith Cheng — Product Lead chat

**Date:** 10 April 2026
**Goal:** Walk out with Keith saying "yes, I'm in" — and a shared idea of what "in" means.
**Style:** Open conversation. Keith talks more than you do. You steer, you don't pitch.

---

## Before you walk in (2 min)

- Breathe. This is a conversation between two people who like the same problem, not a sales call.
- You already have him on the roadmap as Product Lead. He knows. Don't re-introduce the project like he's a stranger.
- One sentence you want him to remember afterwards:
  > *"Driiva is the UK's first insurance product that pays you back for driving well — and it actually works today."*

---

## Open with something human (not a deck)

Pick one, whichever feels true on the day:

- "Thanks for making time — I wanted to talk properly, not just Slack at you."
- "I've been building this on my own for a while and I'm at the point where I need a product brain I actually trust."
- "Before we get into any of it — how's life? How's [thing you know about him]?"

Let him answer. Don't rush to the agenda.

---

## The three things you want him to feel

1. **This is real, not a deck.** Trips record, scores compute, pool share updates, GDPR export works, Sentry is wired, staging is up. Show the app on your phone if he asks.
2. **There's a clear lane for him.** Product ownership — not "help out sometimes."
3. **You respect his time.** You're not asking him to quit his job tomorrow. You're asking him to commit to a shape.

---

## Direct the conversation with four questions

Open platform, but these are your anchors. Ask them in this order — each one pulls him deeper in.

1. **"What would you need to see to believe Driiva can actually be a UK insurance product?"**
   *(Lets him list his objections up front. Write them down. Don't defend — just listen.)*

2. **"If you were running product here, what would you kill first?"**
   *(Tests whether he's thinking like an owner already. If he names something, he's halfway in.)*

3. **"What does the first 90 days look like for you?"**
   *(Shifts the frame from 'should I?' to 'how would I?'. Powerful.)*

4. **"What would make this not worth it for you?"**
   *(The honesty question. Whatever he says — equity, time, clarity, a co-founder title — that's your negotiation.)*

---

## What you can show him if he asks "what's actually built?"

Keep it short. Don't tour the whole repo.

- **Telematics:** Damoov integration live, daily sync Cloud Function running, trips → scores deterministic.
- **Scoring:** Speed / Braking / Accel / Cornering weighted, `computeDrivingScore()` locked as source of truth.
- **Compliance:** ICO registered, FCA sandbox aligned, GDPR export + delete endpoints live, Damoov named as Article 28 processor.
- **Infra:** Firebase + Vercel, Sentry, staging project provisioned, 197 tests passing, CI/CD green.
- **Not yet:** Root Platform credentials, Stripe wired end-to-end, phone pickup detection, XGBoost risk model. *These are the gaps — and they're exactly the kind of thing a Product Lead decides the order of.*

---

## The ask (when the moment comes)

Don't bury it. When the conversation naturally lands, say it plainly:

> "I want you as Product Lead. I've already got you in the roadmap and the docs because that's how I think about it. What I need from you today is a yes in principle — then we figure out shape, time, and equity properly, in writing, this week."

Then **stop talking**. Silence is fine. Let him respond.

---

## If he says yes

- Agree a follow-up within 7 days to nail down: time commitment, equity/comp, title, decision rights, how you two disagree.
- Tell him you'll send a one-pager summarising what you both said. Do it the same day.

## If he says "I need to think"

- Fine. Ask: *"What specifically do you need to think about? If I can answer it now, I will."*
- Agree a date for the follow-up before you leave. Don't leave it open.

## If he says no

- Ask why. Actually listen. Don't argue.
- Ask: *"Is there a version of this you would say yes to?"*
- Thank him. Mean it. Keep the relationship — UK telematics is a small world.

---

## Things to NOT do

- Don't open with the cap table.
- Don't apologise for the state of anything.
- Don't pitch the community pool formula unless he asks.
- Don't promise things you haven't slept on.
- Don't check your phone.

---

## One line for the walk home

Whatever happens, write down the single most surprising thing he said. That's the thing you'll want to remember next week.
